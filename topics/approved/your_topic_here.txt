Can you imagine living with someone who constantly screws up the fabric of reality? That's what it's like to be Rick Sanchez's roommate, Morty Smith. In fact, the whole concept of "family" is put through a meat grinder in episodes like "Pickle Rick", where Rick turns himself into a pickle to avoid going to family therapy.

But what if I told you that Rick's toxic behavior isn't just a product of his intelligence and bad luck? It's actually a coping mechanism for the crushing existential dread that comes with being a multidimensional being. Think about it: every time Rick visits a new dimension, he sees alternate versions of himself, living different lives - some happy, some sad, but all equally meaningless.

So is it any wonder that Rick turns to self-medication and cynical remarks to deal with the weight of infinite possibilities? His antics may be chaotic, but they're also a desperate attempt to impose order on a universe that's fundamentally indifferent to his existence. What happens when Morty finally reaches his breaking point and decides he's had enough of living with the Wubba Lubba Dub-Dub version of reality?
